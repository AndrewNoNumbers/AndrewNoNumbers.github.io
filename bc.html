Designing a more fun way to date

Bruin Connections: UX and Visual Design

Role

As the primary designer on the project, I delivered user research, validated ideas for new features, and developed a cohesive visual design while collaborating with the development team to make it reality.

Timeline

Aug 2016 - Feb 2017

Problem

Customer Need: College students want a dating app that is effective, is fun and original, and is not tedious.

Business Need: College students have lots of dating apps, but they are not satisfied with most of the choices. The app needs a user experience that is differentiated and stands heads and shoulders above others. We are not trying to make an app that is addictive, but one that is effective and fun.

Research

I started with a survey distributed to 164 students at UCLA to attempt to understand the general sentiment toward dating and dating apps in college. I found some interesting insights from this.

There was quite a range of opinions on the question, "What dating app are you particularly impressed by or are particularly active on?" Many mentioned Tinder and that they were not impressed.

About one-third of users have never used a dating app. Two-thirds have.

Most respondents are interested in a long-term relationship.

Majority of respondents know what they are looking for in a romantic partner.

Analytics Data

In addition to surveying our users, I looked to existing Google Analytics data to understand how often people were using the site, what times of day they like to use the site, and what devices they like do use it on.

User Stories

Who are the users, and what are their needs?

Due to the in-depth interviews I conducted for this project, I had enough information to create personas that can help guide further design.

Testing

What do we focus on in our product?

  1. Fewer matches: We found that most users did not have trouble with finding people to meet, but had a harder time getting to know people deeply.
  2. Engagement through email: We found that almost all users already use email. Most said that it would brighten their day to find a message from a potential date in their inbox.
  3. Visual whismy: We found that users reacted best to designs that were not too serious.

UX Challenges

How do you keep people engaged via email?

We decided that the match profile should be the first thing users see because many users return via our email notification. We wanted to display their new match elegantly without just throwing a user’s profile in the user’s face. I decided to integrate a cheerful notification at the top along with some animated icons. Smooth and delightful microinteractions make a difference in users’ long-term retention and happiness.

How do you increase retention of users who have just signed up?

We found in our earlier versions that 23% of users left the site after registering without engaging in any key parts of the product (answering questions, filling out profile, etc.). In response to that, I tweaked the design to add prompts that gently guide first-time users. I also added a counter of the number of people who have answered each question to give users confidence that other people are engaging in this service.

The new knowledge base is a huge improvement and helps both internally and with our users.

Achievements

  1. Planned research, strategy, and user experience for new product
  2. Interviewed 4 users and developed personas
  3. Increased user retention upon signup to 97%
  4. Created visual design and collaborated with developers through Git
