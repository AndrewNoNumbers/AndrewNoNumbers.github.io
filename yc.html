Demonstrating a startup MVP

Oil Supply Chain Project for YCombinator: UX and Visual Design

Role

As the designer on the project, I created prototypes of the client’s ideas and developed them into high-fidelity designs and a video showing how the product works.

Timeline

July 2017 - August 2017

Problem

Customer Need: Various organizations in an oil supply chain will have access to this client. Each user needs to be able to view a large number of categories and transactions quickly.

Business Need: The client is building a blockchain-based technology but wants a sleek GUI client for end users.

How do we create a sleek interface that feels trustworthy for users to manage lots of information?

User Stories

Who are the users, and what are their needs?

  1. As a sourcing specialist at an oil refiner, I need to verify a detail in yesterday’s purchase order. I then add two more purchase orders.
  2. As an upstream sourcing director at a crude oil distributor, I want to scan the different types of contracts my team has negotiated, and add another contract that I negotiated with a refinery in the Midwest.

Prototyping and Testing

A big part of the project is taking lots of complex form information and organizing it hierarchically. I tested two prototypes with different hierarchies and flows with three users. One of the prototypes was easier to pick up by all three of the users.

User Testing Learnings

  1. Testing found that users spend the most time on transaction details and analytics
  2. Users need to quickly know if a contract is in progress or completed.

Achievements

  1. Envisioned product based on client's requirements
  2. Tested prototypes to find the best paradigm
  3. Executed high-fidelity visual design, and demo video
